Old Classroom

Prodedural Surfacing (Maya)

View on Artstation

An old classroom that was long abandoned and left to wither and rust. Everything you see in this environment is surfaced and textured procedurally, with the use of only two textures, one for scratches and one for the tiles on the floor. This scene was set up and lit in Autodesk Maya with the use of Arnold shaders, though the ideas behind procedural shading can be used in Houdini, Unity, or Unreal as well. Game Development (Unity)

Portal Recreation Demo

View on Artstation   |   Github Repository

Portal was a fantastic game and followed by the masterpiece that is portal 2, and I wanted to look a little deeper into how it was made. The portal itself has multiple approaches involving camera effects, room duplicates, or raycasts, and each has their own ups and downs. I decided on camera effects, because they were the easiest to implement in an intuitive way. Each portal has its own second camera that renders the scene and passes the ‘image texture’ to the other portal. The effect worked amazingly, after a good amount of polish, and I would love to dive into the other methods at some point too.

Tank Boss Battle

View on Artstation   |   Github Repository

A boss battle! With tanks! What could be better? I've long been a fan of an old and simple game called Wii Play Tanks, and wanted to implement some of those ideas into a game. The perfect opportunity came as a university project, where we had to build a simple tank controller and a boss that could move and attack. I expanded on this idea and built a simple intro cutscene to build up the boss and gave him multiple stages to make it more progressive. On a more technical level, I implemented the observer pattern for more efficient game references, the state pattern for cleaner AI, and object pooling (for both sound effects and the bullets) for better performance.
