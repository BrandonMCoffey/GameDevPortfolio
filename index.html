Analysis of Earthbound (1995)

       Earthbound, known as Mother II in Japan, redefined the very popular genre of role playing games (RPGs) with their unique, down to earth story approach. Development of the game was a joint effort between Ape Inc and HAL Laboratories, led by Shigesato Itoi. The developers’ intent with this RPG was to get the player invested in a story through the use of emotional storytelling and characters with strong and quirky personalities. Itoi “wanted to create a game (EarthBound) with real characters; characters whom players would recognize in the people around them” (Mato). Earthbound achieves these effects on the player through unconventional level design, text dialog boxes that are hidden everywhere, and personal narrative.

       The level design in Earthbound is unconventional and diverse. The world is large with set styles for different areas while still including minor details everywhere. The developers focused on worldbuilding more than anything else. This enhances the game by broadening the experience of the game to enjoy across different levels. There is no hub world or standard game area. This way, the level feels more like an open world, where the player can explore or continue the story as they like. Eventually, as the game progresses, odd visual and artistic choices are also taken, especially at the end with the battle against Giygas, the final boss. Through all the world design and artistic choices, Itoi’s goal is to make the player feel emotions such as happiness and satisfaction as well as distraught and loneliness.

       At the core of the game, the use of the text box is widespread, as it was manipulated and emphasized across the game to better illustrate the idea presented, along with clever writing to match. The use of text boxes to display information or simple dialog is common, but to hide dialog all over the environment throughout the game is unique. This is an interesting choice, as most RPGs are more streamlined and appeal solely to fantasy, while this is surreal and feels like an entirely different genre. However, Earthbound’s text dialogue is on a deeper level than just text, as the writing is impactful, always relevant or humorous, and most importantly, it does not just convey information, but successfully does so in a unique way each and every time. Stuff like saving, battle dialogue, or tips and hits, are often memorable, ridiculous, or off-putting, which is such an incredible touch of language that works to distinguish this game from other RPGs.

       Throughout the game, Earthbound has a directed, yet open narrative. Much of the game is not the main story; it is supported by all of the characters and towns and dialogue. The narrative is often quirky and personal, leading the player to become more emotionally invested into the story, which is a core element of the game. This is mostly due to the director, Itoi, where he would present odd ideas and eventually “people would start chiming in with other similar ideas of their own. Those links of reckless wildness are what the Mother games are built on” (Schreier). Such a creative open dialogue within the developmental team paid off in a unique and intriguing game that became so much more than just a cut and dry RPG. This creative “wildness” is directly applied to the narrative and to environmental features, as described before with hidden text boxes, which emphasize a non-linear story.

       The atmosphere, text box usage, and the narrative are all closely tied together and work to benefit each other to make Earthbound into a game that inspired so many after it. Earthbound “remains an underappreciated video game franchise with a dedicated cult following, but with it, some of the most popular indie games today would not exist” (Jensen). One game that was directly influenced by Earthbound is the 2015's Undertale, by its unique means of how RPGs are played. The diverse text box is used heavily and story and worldbuilding is everything in the game. Both games are meant to be an intimate experience for the player, where they are invested into the immersive world presented in the game by the non-linear level design and quirky characters who are filled with diverse personalities.
